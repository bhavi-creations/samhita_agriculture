Chemical Agriculture

Chemical agriculture relies heavily on synthetic fertilizers, pesticides, and herbicides to enhance crop yields and control pests. While it supports large-scale food production, it often leads to soil degradation, water pollution, and long-term health impacts. This method prioritizes productivity over ecological balance, raising concerns about sustainability and food safety.

Natural agriculture is a sustainable farming approach that avoids synthetic inputs. It focuses on enriching soil health using compost, crop rotation, and biodiversity. Natural farming practices are gaining momentum as a safe and eco-friendly alternative.
